There is a monkey which can walk around on a planar grid.

How many points, the sum of whose digits is less than or equal to 19, can the monkey access if it starts at (0, 0), including (0, 0) itself?

Using a x grid.
0


     
Rules for Monkey Movement:
  1. The monkey can move one space at a time left, right, up or down.
  2. For example, the point (59, 79) is inaccessible because 5 + 9 + 7 + 9 = 30, which is greater than 19.
  3. Points where the sum of the digits of the absolute value of the x coordinate plus the sum of the digits of the absolute value of the y coordinate are lesser than or equal to 19 are accessible to the monkey.
  4. That is, from (x, y) the monkey can go to (x+1, y), (x-1, y), (x, y+1), and (x, y-1).
  5. Another example: the point (-5, -7) is accessible because abs(-5) + abs(-7) = 5 + 7 = 12, which is less than 19.
